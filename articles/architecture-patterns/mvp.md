> minimum viable product — the smallest thing you can ship that doesn't embarrass you. sometimes you ship it anyway.

## real talk

MVP means build the least possible thing that proves your idea works. Not the full vision. Not the polished version. The version that answers: "will anyone actually use this?"

The word "minimum" does a lot of heavy lifting. PMs think it means "without the nice-to-haves." Engineers think it means "without half the features." Users think it means "broken."

Also worth knowing: MVP means Most Valuable Player in sports. Context matters. You will use the wrong one in the wrong meeting exactly once.

## used in the wild

> "forget the dashboard for now, we just need an MVP to show investors next week. hardcode whatever you have to."
>
> — PM, every sprint, forever

## why it matters

Companies waste months building features nobody asked for. MVP forces the question: what is the single thing that matters? Ship that. Learn. Iterate. The alternative is six months of work thrown away because you built the wrong thing beautifully.
